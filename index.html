Food waste guru lauded by Meghan Markle

A businesswoman lauded by the Duchess of Sussex in Vogue magazine is a former boss of Wonga, the 'legal loan shark' firm vilified for preying on desperate borrowers, The Mail on Sunday can reveal. As guest editor of the high-fashion magazine, the Duchess introduces Tessa Clarke, who has set up a food waste app OLIO, as someone 'you may want to know', whom she had 'met with discreetly last year.

In 2014 Clarke – who posed for the latest edition of Vogue in an outfit worth £1,700 – even appeared on television to apologise for fake legal letters the company had sent before she joined, which threatened hard-up customers who were struggling to make repayments.
